Portfolio

Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research

September 2022 - May 2024

As a Graduate Student Researcher in the Berkeley Speech Group, I focused on Differentiable Digital Signal Processing for my Master's research, developing a novel real-time data efficient machine learning model for articulatory synthesis. By engineering a streamlined architecture requiring only 0.4M parameters (vs. 9M baseline), I improved Word Error Rate by 1.63% (to 6.67%) and Mean Opinion Score by 0.16 (to 3.74) while achieving 4.9x inference speedup on CPU compared to state-of-the-art models. This performance improvement enabled real-time applications while maintaining speech quality.

This included developing a spectral analysis system leveraging Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) to detect and characterize reaction wheel bearing faults, as well as creating a comprehensive test automation suite for flight component qualification that integrated hardware control interfaces and data acquisition systems.

MIT Lincoln Laboratory

May 2021 - July 2021

As a Research Intern at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, I led the design and construction of a custom 4-antenna array system for tracking Lincoln Experimental Satellites (LES), achieving successful signal capture at 230 MHz from multiple satellites. By developing and optimizing a Blind Adaptive Beamforming algorithm, I was able to isolate and identify satellite signals amid complex co-channel interference, improving signal-to-noise ratio by 30%. The work included implementing end-to-end integration testing using phase shifters and RF components to simulate antenna array geometry, with data capture and processing done through GNURadio, Python, and MATLAB.

QubeSat

Fall 2019 - 2021

As part of the mechanical subteam at Space Technologies at Cal, I designed the structure and deployment mechanism while integrating the payload and sensors for the CubeSat, using SolidWorks and other software to simulate and test the design. Beyond the mechanical work, I also wrote the flight software for the mission. Our goal was to design the first quantum gyroscope fit for space and deploy it on a CubeSat through NASA's CubeSat Launch Initiative—a revolutionary technology that offers better precision and accuracy compared to traditional MEMS gyroscopes, making it ideal for deep space missions. QubeSat was successfully flown as part of NASA's ELaNa 41 mission. Click here to check out the website!

Mood for Zoom

Spring 2020

During quarantine, my friends and I noticed that professors had trouble engaging with students, so we built Mood for Zoom for LA Hacks—a real-time aggregate mood visualization tool to help professors, educators, and meeting leaders "read the room" over Zoom. The project leveraged the Google Cloud Vision API to read emotions from faces, PyAutoGUI to continuously capture screenshots, and TkInter to display the visualization. We were awarded 2nd Place Overall and an honorable mention for Hack Using Machine Learning. Click here to check out the Devpost!

Senate Watch

Spring 2020

The average American knows very little about their representatives and whether their own views are being properly represented, which is why we created Senate Watch—a tool that scrapes the official senate.gov website to display voting patterns based on party loyalty. We used beautiful-soup and requests for scraping, Python for data processing, and jQuery/JavaScript for the frontend. Click here to check out the Devpost!

Crawler Robot

Summer 2018

At COSMOS in UCSD, I worked on designing a "walking" robot from scratch, ultimately settling on an insectoid design. My partner and I designed the circuitry, wrote code for a Raspberry Pi and Adafruit PWM driver, and modeled and 3D printed the chassis. In the final time-based race, our robot beat the other competitors by a margin of 50%.
